What I'm thinking about
—

Agentic AI is a distribution problem, not a model problem.

The hardest part isn't the intelligence — it's trust, control surfaces, and the humans who have to stay in the loop without slowing everything down.

—

Vertical SaaS for trade industries is still massively underserved.

Landscapers, HVAC techs, and pest control operators run real businesses on spreadsheets and gut feel. The right product isn't a simplified Salesforce — it's something built *for them*, not adapted from enterprise tools.

—

The best PMs think in systems, not features.

A feature is a guess. A system is a theory about how the world works. I'd rather be wrong about a system than right about a feature — because systems teach you something.

—

Localization is a product strategy, not a localization problem.

Deciding which markets to serve, in which languages, at what quality level — that's a product decision with compounding returns. Most teams treat it as a translation problem and wonder why it fails.
